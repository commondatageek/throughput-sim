How to read this diagram

Each colored band represents items currently in that stage. The band's vertical height at any point equals the WIP in that stage. The horizontal distance between the top edge of a band and its bottom edge approximates the average time items spend there (Little's Law: WIP = throughput × cycle time).

Ideal shape: straight, parallel lines with constant-width bands. Straight means a steady rate; parallel means inflow equals outflow at every boundary so no queue is accumulating. A widening band signals a bottleneck; a narrowing band means that stage is draining. A flattening top line means fewer new issues are being created.
